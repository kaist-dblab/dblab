A digital library is an advanced library system that stores information in the digital form and that provides facilities to search and access the information. A core part of the digital library is the DBMS. The DBMS stores information in various forms and provides efficient management and fast search of information. The ODYSSEUS DBMS developed at our laboratory is best suited for the digital library engine. ODYSSEUS provides fast IR search for multiple fields. This capability is provided through tight coupling of the IR capability with the DBMS. Z39.50 server for ODYSSEUS has also been developed. Z39.50 is a standard protocol for digital libraries. Currently, various digital library service systems are being developed using ODYSSEUS. Digital library systems currently in service using ODYSSEUS are as follows :

 

  • KAIST Digital Science Library (http://library.kaist.ac.kr)
  • The Club Search System of the NeoWiz SayClub Bulletin Board (http://finder.sayclub.com/finder_club.nwz)
  • Information Center for Mathematical Sciences : Theatise Search System (http://icms.kaist.ac.kr/index.html)
  • The Supreme Public Prosecutor's Office : Judicial Affair and Public Prosecutor General Information Retrieval System
  • Korea Ginseng and Tobacco Research Institute : Research Information Database System
